INTERACTIVE EXPERIENCE RESEARCH PROJECT - RICH BROWN

By Tien Bui (s4165740)

  • Question 1: What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?
When first opening the Rich Brown website, the first thing that caught my eye was the movement of all the elements within the website and how the designer of this interactive experience used them to unify with one another, creating satisfying flowing movement. For example, the animation used for text and the background video.
  • Question 2: Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.
With my two minute experience, the actions I did interacting with the website is:

- Continuously srolling up and down to watch the movement of the elements presented on the screen.

- Scrolling through the page at a very fast pace.

- Having a difficult time figuring out where my cursor is, so I'd constantly hover around trying to figure out where my mouse is at.

- When on the list of projects the designer worked on, I hovered around the screen a lot to watch the images move and the background colour change.

  • Question 3: What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?
I spent most of my time on the opening start screen, as previously stated, I spent most of my time there by playing with it scrolling back and forth to see the animation for the text and video move, since I found that to be the most interactive and engaging throughout the experience.
  • Question 4: What was the most common action in your two-minute interaction with the experience?
The most common thing I did throughout the experience was scroll up and down to watch each part move. I found everything to flow and move so satisfyingly that I got a bit too lost and caught up in the movement rather than the information it was trying to display.
  • Question 5: What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?
I think the designer did a good job of displaying their portfolio, as it allows the user, which in this case, is me, to get a glimpse of their personality and work through the interactive experience. Their art style is maintained and displayed even through the website, which is relevant when you create a portfolio, demonstrating heir skills and creativity.
  • Question 6: How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?
Through the opening screen. When you first visit the site, it shows the designer themselves, while displaying a short “about me” for the user to read. As you progress through the site, it takes you to a section where they display their works, allowing the user to understand the designer and to be able to see their works and projects.
  • Question 7: What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)
I think the experience is good for short-term interactions. There are very limited different options and screens the user can be directed to, keeping it short and simple (information wise). The visuals are what carry the experience, as portfolios are usually looked at for a short amount of time, especially if its being looked over by art agents/directors, etc. The user can fully immerse themselves and get a deep understanding of Rich Brown from a short scroll. It can be good to revisit if you're trying to look back on more projects, but having to sit through the opening animations could get a bit annoying.
  • Question 8: How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?
I personally do not think there is anything in the interactive experience that clearly encourages user to revisit the site. It's more so a one time visit site for agents and directors to get an understanding of Rich Brown as a designer just based off first impressions since they are known for quick reviews.
  • Question 9: What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?
The experience references an animated/videograph form as every single element within the site is animated/has some sort of movement to it.
